Isabelle Lorge


I recently completed a PhD in Theoretical and Applied Linguistics at the University of Cambridge with Dr. Napoleon Katsos. My research focused on pragmatic (context-driven) inferences for word learning in bilingual and monolingual children using human data and Bayesian models. (e.g., 'pick the wet gorp' in image below)


In April 2017, I interned at Microsoft Research Cambridge and collaborated with Alex Taylor from the Human Experience and Design (HXD) group and Nate Kushman from the Machine Intelligence and Perception (MIP) group on identification and understanding of context-specific features in dialog using deep learning networks.


During my studies, I conducted several Natural Language Processing projects, including anomaly detection in legal documents (Faculty fellowship 14th) and investigating the combination of symbolic and probabilistic reasoning for Natural Language Understanding.


As a psycholinguist and a pragmatician, I am interested in both the formal and cognitive aspects of language processing, and where they potentially meet (i.e., artificial intelligence and computational models that try to describe linguistic behaviour).


I currently research NLP for portfolio optimisation at Arabesque AI. My research areas include sentiment analysis in news and social media, unsupervised learning and knowledge graphs.
