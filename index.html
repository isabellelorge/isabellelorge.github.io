Isabelle Lorge


I'm a third year PhD student in Theoretical and Applied Linguistics at the University of Cambridge. My supervisor is Dr. Napoleon Katsos. My research focuses on pragmatic (context-driven) inferences for word learning in bilingual and monolingual children using human data and Bayesian models.


Last April I interned at Microsoft Research Cambridge and collaborated with Alex Taylor from the Human Experience and Design (HXD) group and Nate Kushman from the Machine Intelligence and Perception (MIP) group on identification and understanding of context-specific features in dialog using deep learning networks.


As a psycholinguist and a pragmatician, I am interested in both the formal and cognitive aspects of language processing, and where they potentially meet (i.e., artificial intelligence and computational models that try to describe linguistic behaviour).
